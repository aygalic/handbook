    Sampling Theory¶

    Population vs Sample¶

    The foundation of statistical inference lies in the relationship between populations and samples:

    • Population: The complete set of all elements we want to study
    • Sample: A subset of the population used to make inferences

    Mathematical Notation¶

    • Population parameters: , , ,
    • Sample statistics: , x, s, p

    The relationship between population parameters and sample statistics can be expressed through expected values: * E[x] = (unbiased estimator of mean) * E[s] = (unbiased estimator of variance)

    Sampling Distributions¶

    The sampling distribution describes the probability distribution of a statistic across all possible samples of size n.

    Standard Error¶

    Mathematical Definition¶

    For any statistic : SE() = Var()

    Common Forms¶

    1. Mean: SE(x) = /n
    2. Proportion: SE(p) = (p(1-p)/n)
    3. Difference of Means: SE(x - x) = (/n + /n)

    When population parameters are unknown, we use sample estimates: s/n, (p(1-p)/n), etc.
